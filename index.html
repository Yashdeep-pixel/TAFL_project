The Pumping Lemma states that for any regular language $L$, there exists a pumping length $p$ such that every string $s \in L$ with $|s| \geq p$ can be split into $s = xyz$ where $|y| \geq 1$, $|xy| \leq p$, and $xy^i z \in L$ for all $i \geq 0$. 📖 Formal Statement

Let $L$ be a regular language. Then $\exists\, p \geq 1$ (the pumping length) such that $\forall s \in L$ with $|s| \geq p$, $s$ can be written as $s = xyz$ satisfying:

  • ① $|y| \geq 1$  — the pumpable segment is non-empty
  • ② $|xy| \leq p$  — pump occurs in the first $p$ characters
  • ③ $\forall i \geq 0 : xy^i z \in L$  — all pumped strings remain in $L$

⚠ If ANY choice of $xyz$ leads to $xy^i z \notin L$ for some $i$, the language is NOT regular.
